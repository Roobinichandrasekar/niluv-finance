Personal Loan
How to Get a Personal Loan in India — A Complete Guide
Personal loans are one of the most accessible forms of credit in India, requiring no collateral and disbursed within 24–72 hours for eligible applicants.
Documents Required
  • Aadhaar Card & PAN Card (KYC)
  • Last 3 months salary slips (salaried)
  • Last 6 months bank statements
  • Form 16 / ITR (last 2 years)
  • Passport-size photograph
  • Employment letter / offer letter
Who Can Apply?
  • Salaried employees aged 21–60
  • Min. monthly income ₹15,000+
  • Min. 1 year of employment
  • CIBIL score 700 and above

Whether you're a manufacturer, trader, or service provider, business loans can provide the working capital needed to scale your operations confidently.

Personal Loan Guide
Personal Loan vs Credit Card — Which is Better for Your Needs?

When you face an unexpected expense, two common options are a personal loan or a credit card. Understanding the difference helps you choose the most cost-effective option.

When to Choose a Personal Loan

  • Large one-time expenses (₹1 lakh and above)
  • When you need structured EMI repayment over 1–5 years
  • Lower interest rate compared to credit card revolving balance
  • Consolidating multiple debts into one monthly payment

Interest Rate Overview

Personal loan interest rates in India typically range from 10.5% to 24% per annum depending on your credit score, income, employer category, and the lender. A CIBIL score above 750 significantly improves your chances of getting the best rates.

💡 Niluv Finance Tip: Always check your CIBIL score before applying. A soft enquiry won't hurt your score, and knowing your score helps us recommend the right lender for you.

How to Improve Your Loan Eligibility

  • Maintain a healthy CIBIL score (700+)
  • Keep existing EMIs below 40% of monthly income
  • Avoid multiple loan applications simultaneously
  • Ensure salary is credited regularly via bank transfer
Business Loan Guide
How to Prepare Your Business for a Loan Application

Many business owners lose out on timely funding because their financial documentation isn't loan-ready. A little preparation can go a long way toward faster approval and better interest rates.

Step 1: Get Your Books in Order

Lenders primarily look at your ITR, GST returns, and bank statements. Ensure all returns are filed on time and income is reflected accurately. Discrepancies between ITR and bank statements raise red flags.

Step 2: Understand the Types of Business Loans

  • Term Loan: Fixed amount, fixed EMI, for capital expenditure
  • Working Capital Loan: Revolving credit for day-to-day operations
  • MSME / Government Scheme Loans: CGTMSE, Mudra, etc. for smaller businesses
  • Overdraft Facility: Flexible credit line against your current account
💡 Niluv Finance Tip: Contact us before applying anywhere. We'll assess your profile and recommend the lender most likely to approve you at the best rate — saving you unnecessary CIBIL hits.

What Lenders Look For

  • 3+ years of stable business vintage
  • Consistent monthly bank credits
  • Low cheque bounce history
  • Positive net profit in ITR
  • Clear GST filings without large gaps
Home Loan Guide
5 Things to Know Before Applying for a Home Loan

Buying a home is one of the most significant financial decisions of your life. Knowing these five things before you apply can save you time, money, and stress.

1. Loan-to-Value (LTV) Ratio

Banks typically fund 75–90% of the property's value depending on loan amount. You will need to arrange the remaining 10–25% as down payment from your own savings.

2. Fixed vs Floating Interest Rate

  • Fixed Rate: EMI stays constant — good for budgeting, but usually higher
  • Floating Rate: Tied to RBI repo rate — changes over time, usually cheaper long-term

3. Legal Due Diligence is Crucial

The lender will conduct a legal check on the property. Ensure the sale deed, EC, approved plan, and tax receipts are in order before you approach us. Issues here delay approvals significantly.

💡 Niluv Finance Tip: We can help you do a preliminary document check before submitting to the lender — identifying issues early saves weeks of delay.

4. Check Your FOIR (Fixed Obligation to Income Ratio)

Banks allow a maximum of 50–65% of your gross income toward all EMIs combined. If you have existing loans, factor in how the new home loan EMI affects this ratio.

5. Balance Transfer Option

If you already have a home loan at a high interest rate, it can be transferred to another lender at a lower rate. Even a 0.5% reduction over 20 years saves lakhs in interest.

LAP Guide
Loan Against Property — The Smart Way to Access Large Funds

LAP (Loan Against Property) is one of the most underutilized credit products in India. Many property owners don't realize that their idle asset can unlock significant capital at lower interest rates.

Key Advantages of LAP

  • Interest rates typically 9–13% — much lower than personal loans
  • Higher loan amounts — up to ₹5 crore or more
  • Longer repayment tenures (up to 15–20 years)
  • Can be used for any purpose — business, education, medical, etc.

Common Misconceptions

  • Myth: "I'll lose my property" — Fact: You continue to own and use the property; it's only a security charge.
  • Myth: "LAP takes too long" — Fact: With proper documentation, it can be processed in 10–20 business days.
  • Myth: "Only residential property is accepted" — Fact: Commercial and industrial properties also qualify.
💡 Niluv Finance Tip: If your business needs more than ₹30 lakhs, LAP is almost always a better option than an unsecured business loan — lower rate, longer tenure, bigger amount.
Education Loan Guide
Funding Your Child's Education — A Guide for Parents

Education costs in India and abroad have risen significantly over the last decade. An education loan helps bridge the gap between your savings and the total cost of education without derailing your other financial goals.

What Expenses Does an Education Loan Cover?

  • Tuition and examination fees
  • Hostel and accommodation costs
  • Books, laptop, and study materials
  • Travel expenses (for overseas studies)
  • Caution deposit (subject to conditions)

Tax Benefit Under Section 80E

Interest paid on education loans qualifies for tax deduction under Section 80E of the Income Tax Act — with no upper limit on the amount, for up to 8 consecutive assessment years. This makes education loans particularly tax-efficient.

💡 Niluv Finance Tip: Many parents don't know that the Section 80E deduction applies to the co-applicant (parent) as well — meaning you can claim the deduction even if the student isn't earning yet.

Moratorium Period

Education loans typically have a moratorium period — you don't need to pay EMI during the course duration plus 6–12 months after completion. Repayment begins after the student starts earning, making it very manageable.
